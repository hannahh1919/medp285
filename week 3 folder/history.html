Fun Facts

Fact 1

This hue is closely associated with environmentalism and sustainability, highlighting a dedication to conserving our planet and fostering a harmonious relationship with nature.

Fact 2

Forest green represents growth, renewal, and the unending cycle of life.

Fact 3

It is often used as a symbol of stability

Fact 4

Used in military camouflage due to its ability to blend with woodland environments

Fact 5

Pairs well with bright red, creating a Christmas-like aesthetic, or can be used with blues and other greens to avoid this association

Fact 6

In China, green jade represents virtue and beauty.

Fact 7

One of the rare colors of forest green is smaragdine which is a deep green, after the Latin word for “emerald”

Fact 8

Forest green is a representation of the average color of the leaves of the trees of a temperate zone deciduous forest

Fact 9

The first recorded use of forest green as a color name in English was in 1810.

Fact 10

Pair forest green with neutral colors like beige, gray, or white to create a harmonious and sophisticated look.
